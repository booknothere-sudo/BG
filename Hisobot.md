# irrigation #
Different environments and crops require different watering methods. The four most common types include:
    1. Drip (or Trickle) Irrigation
This is one of the most efficient water-delivery methods available. Water is delivered directly to the root zone of the plant through a network of valves, pipes, tubing, and emitters.

Efficiency: Up to 90% (very low evaporation and runoff).

Best For: Row crops, orchards, vineyards, raised garden beds, and arid regions.

Pros: Conserves water, reduces weed growth (since water is localized), and minimizes soil erosion.

2. Sprinkler Irrigation
Water is piped to one or more central locations within the field and distributed by overhead high-pressure sprinking devices or guns. It mimics natural rainfall.

Types: Center pivot (large circular fields), lateral move, and traditional pop-up lawn sprinklers.

Best For: Large-scale agricultural fields, lawns, and golf courses.

Cons: High evaporation rates on windy or hot days; can promote fungal diseases if plant foliage stays wet too long.
